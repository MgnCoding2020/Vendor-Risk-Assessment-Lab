# Vendor Risk Assessment Glossary

This glossary explains key terms used throughout the Vendor Risk Assessment Lab. Definitions are written in practical terms to support understanding of the vendor risk assessment process.

---

## Vendor
A third-party company that provides services, software, or support to the organization. Vendors can introduce risk by processing data, accessing systems, or supporting operations.

---

## Third-Party Risk
Risk introduced to the business through external companies that provide services or products. This can include data exposure, operational disruption, or a security weakness in the vendor's environment.

---

## Vendor Risk Assessment
A structured review that evaluates the security, operational, and compliance risks associated with using a vendor. It typically includes vendor intake, inherent risk scoring, security due diligence, and a final risk decision.

---

## Vendor Intake
The first step in the vendor risk process, where information about the vendor and its services is collected. Intake helps determine the level of review required, based on inherent risk.

---

## Inherent Risk
The level of risk associated with a vendor **before** reviewing the vendor's security controls. It is driven by factors such as data sensitivity, system access, operational dependency, and regulatory exposure.

---

## Residual Risk
The remaining level of risk **after** reviewing security controls and applying mitigation. Residual risk is used to determine whether a vendor can be approved or requires further remediation.

---

## Vendor Due Diligence
The process of reviewing a vendor's security practices, policies, and controls — for example, independent audit reports, access controls, encryption, and incident response procedures.

---

## Security Controls
Safeguards or processes that protect systems, data, and operations. Examples include access controls, encryption, monitoring, and vulnerability management.

---

## Privileged Access
Access that allows a user to perform administrative or high-impact actions within a system. Privileged access must be carefully managed, because misuse or compromise of these accounts can cause significant damage.

---

## Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
Information that can identify a specific individual, such as name, address, government ID number, or banking details. PII requires protection to prevent identity theft and privacy harm.

---

## SOC 2
An independent audit report on a service provider's security controls. A **Type I** report assesses control design at a single point in time; a **Type II** report assesses operating effectiveness over a period.

---

## Service Level Agreement (SLA)
A contractual commitment defining expected service levels — for example, a breach-notification SLA stating how quickly a vendor must report a security incident.

---

## Risk Mitigation
Actions taken to reduce or control identified risks, such as requiring stronger controls, limiting vendor access, or adding monitoring.

---

## Risk Acceptance
A management decision to accept a known risk when mitigation is not practical or the risk level is considered acceptable.

---

## Risk Decision
The final determination made after a vendor risk assessment. Common outcomes are approve, approve with conditions, or reject.

---

## POA&M (Plan of Action and Milestones)
A tracked list of identified weaknesses, their planned remediation, owners, and status. In this project, vendor findings roll up to the GRC program's POA&M under control **SA-9 (External System Services)**.

---
