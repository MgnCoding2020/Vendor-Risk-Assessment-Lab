# Vendor Risk Assessment Glossary

This glossary is to help explain key terms used throughout the **Vendor-Risk_assessment-Lab**.

The definitions are written in practical terms to support understanding of the vendor risk assessment process.

----

## Vendor
A third-party company that could provide services, software, or support to the organization.

Vendors can introduce risk by processing data, accessing systems, or supporting operations.

---

## Third-Party Risk
Risk is introduced to the business through external companies that provide services or products.

This risk could include data exposure, operational disruption, or a security weakness in the vendor's environment.

---

## Vendor Risk Assessment
A structured review is performed to evaluate the security, operational, and compliance risks associated with using a vendor.

This process typically includes vendor intake, risk scoring, security due diligence, and a final risk decision.

## Vendor Intake
The initial step in the vendor risk process is to collect information about the vendor and the services they provide.

Vendor Intake helps determine the appropriate level of vendor risk review required based on the inherent risk.

----

## Inherent Risk
The level of risk associated with a vendor **before reviewing the vendor's security controls**.

Inherent risk is determined, but not limited to, the type of service provided, the sensitivity of the data involved, system access requirements, and the importance of the vendor to business operations.

-----

## Residual Risk
The remaining level of risk **after reviewing security controls and mitigation efforts**.

The residual risk is used to determine whether the vendor can be approved or requires additional remediation.

----

## Vendor Due Diligence
The process of reviewing a vendor's security practices, policies, and controls.

Examples include reviewing documentation, security reports, access controls, and incident response procedures.

----

## Security Controls
Safeguards or processes implemented to protect systems, data, and operations.

Examples include access controls, encryption, monitoring, and vulnerability management.

-----

## Privileged Access
Access that allows a user to perform administrative or high-impact actions within a system.

Privileged access must be carefully managed because misuse or compromise of these accounts can cause significant damage.

----

## Risk Mitigation
Actions taken to reduce or control identified risks.

Examples include requiring stronger security controls, limiting vendor access, or implementing additional monitoring.

-----

## Risk Acceptance
A decision made by management to accept a known risk  when mitigation is not practical, or the risk level is considered acceptable.

----

## Risk Decision
The final determination is made after the vendor risk assessment.

Common outcomes include approving the vendor, approving with conditions, or rejecting the vendor relationship.

----
